# 1\. Audience Fit

## 1.1 Primary ICP: Aesthetic Millennial Mom

-   **Context:** Mobile-first, juggling micro-moments of peace. Values "dopamine decor" (cute, trendy, joyful) but hates visual noise.
    
-   **Trigger:** "I need 20 minutes to cook/breathe." Looking for a non-screen "electronic babysitter alternative."
    
-   **Friction:** Pinterest doom-scrolling leads to dead links, blurry JPEGs, or ad-heavy sites that are unsafe/frustrating to navigate with kids.
    
-   **Core Need:** Instant gratification. She wants to see "cute capybara," click print, and hand it to her child in under 60 seconds.
    
-   **Aesthetic Bar:** "Fridge-worthy." If it's ugly clip art, she won't print it. It reflects her taste.
    

## 1.2 Secondary ICP: Pediatric OT/SLP

-   **Context:** Desktop/Tablet workflow. Planning weekly sessions for multiple kids with varying needs (motor delays, focus issues).
    
-   **Trigger:** "I need 10 'Space' themed sheets that are actually usable for low-motor-control patients."
    
-   **Friction:** Most "cute" coloring pages have tiny details that frustrate motor-delayed kids. Most "accessible" pages look clinical and boring.
    
-   **Core Need:** Functional clarity. Thick lines (for heavy markers/weak grip), distinct shapes, no distracting background noise.
    
-   **Aesthetic Bar:** Professional utility. It must look like a tool, not just a toy.
    

## 1.3 Shared Needs + Tensions (and how the design resolves them)

-   **Tension:** "Cute" usually means "cluttered/detailed." "Accessible" usually means "boring/clinical."
    
-   **Resolution:** The "Bold & Easy" style. Thick lines satisfy the OT's need for motor accessibility while delivering the "chunky/kawaii" aesthetic the Mom loves.
    
-   **Tension:** "Free" usually means "spammy." "Paid" creates friction.
    
-   **Resolution:** The "Trusted Studio" model. A calm, ad-free UI justifies the subscription by saving time and mental energy.

# 4\. Art Direction System

## 4.1 Visual Style Attributes

-   **We are:** A boutique studio. **Not:** A warehouse.
    
-   **We are:** Bold and chunky. **Not:** Delicate or sketchy.
    
-   **We are:** Calm and organized. **Not:** Busy or loud.
    
-   **We are:** Tactile and soft. **Not:** Flat or corporate.
    
-   **We are:** Playful (Coral/Lilac). **Not:** Childish (Primary Red/Blue).
    
-   **We are:** Vector-sharp. **Not:** Pixel-fuzzy.
    
-   **We are:** Mobile-first thumbnails. **Not:** Desktop-first text lists.
    
-   **We are:** Generous with white space. **Not:** Crammed to the fold.
    
-   **We are:** "Soft Pop" depth. **Not:** Hard material design.
    
-   **We are:** Human-curated. **Not:** Algorithm-vomit.
    

## 4.2 Color Strategy

-   **Primary: Deep Teal (#0F766E):** Used for primary actions (buttons), active states, and strong brand links. Signals calm authority and trust (clinical nod).
    
-   **Secondary: Warm Coral (#F97360):** Used for "Dopamine" accents—"New" badges, call-to-action highlights, playful illustrations. High energy but soft.
    
-   **Accent: Lilac Tint (#F3EFFF):** Used for backgrounds, decorative shapes, and subtle highlights. Softens the stark white/black contrast.
    
-   **Neutral: Ink Black (#111827):** The "Line Art" color. Used for all text, icons, and borders.
    
-   **Neutral: White (#FFFFFF):** The "Paper." Card backgrounds, input fields, modal surfaces.
    

## 4.3 Typography Strategy

-   **Headings: Lora (Serif)**
    
    -   _Why:_ Evokes the "Publisher/Press" authority. Feels like a storybook or a high-end magazine.
        
    -   _Usage:_ H1 (Page Titles), H2 (Section Headers), Featured Cards.
        
    -   _Style:_ Medium (500) or Bold (700). Tight tracking (-0.01em or -0.02em) for elegance.
        
-   **Body/UI: Plus Jakarta Sans (Sans-serif)**
    
    -   _Why:_ Modern, geometric, highly legible at small sizes. Matches the "vector" aesthetic.
        
    -   _Usage:_ Navigation, Buttons, Meta-data (tags), Body text.
        
    -   _Style:_ Regular (400), Medium (500), Bold (700). Standard tracking.
        

## 4.4 Shape + Line Language

-   **Corners:** Generous rounding. `12px` for small elements (tags, inputs), `16px` for cards, `24px` for modals/feature sections. No sharp corners (except the "paper dog-ear" icon concept).
    
-   **Borders:** Thick and distinct. `2px` solid borders on inputs, secondary buttons, and key containers. Echoes the "Bold Line Art" product promise.
    
-   **Icons:** Thick stroke (`2px`), rounded caps. Style: Lucid / Feather / Heroicons Outline (bold). Color: Ink Black. No filled icons unless active state.
    

## 4.5 Imagery Rules

-   **Thumbnails:** The hero of the site. Must be high-contrast black lines on white. No gray backgrounds.
    
    -   _Constraint:_ Aspect ratio ~1:1.41 (A4 paper shape).
        
    -   _Detail:_ Show the full page, but allow a "zoom" hover to see line quality.
        
-   **Photography:** Use strictly for "Lifestyle Proof." Real printed pages, colored with markers, on a clean table (wood/white). No generic stock photos of smiling kids. Show hands working.
    
-   **Negative Space:** Crucial. The UI is the frame; the art is the picture. Don't crowd the thumbnails.
    

## 4.6 Motion + Microinteractions

-   **Principle:** "Subtle Dopamine."
    
-   **Hover:** Cards lift slightly (Elevation 1 -> 2) and the title color shifts to Deep Teal.
    
-   **Click:** Buttons scale down (`0.98`) for tactile feedback.
    
-   **Loading:** Use a playful "scribble" animation or a "stacking paper" loader instead of a generic spinner.
    
-   **Transitions:** Snappy (200-300ms). No slow fades.
    

# 5\. Website UX Translation

## 5.1 Homepage Structure

1.  **Hero:** "Curated Coloring for Modern Moms." Split screen: Text left (Lora H1), Hero Image right (Spread of coloring pages or a "fan" of papers).
    
    -   _CTA:_ "Unlock the Vault" (Primary).
        
2.  **Problem/Agitation:** "Stop searching for ugly clip art." Simple visual comparison: "Them (Pixelated/Busy)" vs "Us (Vector/Bold)."
    
3.  **The Vault Preview:** Grid of 6 high-performing assets (e.g., "Trending Now"). Shows variety.
    
4.  **Value Prop Grid:** 3 Icons (Therapy-Grade, Dopamine, Calm). Short blurbs.
    
5.  **How it Works:** 1. Join ($5), 2. Print, 3. Peace.
    
6.  **Pricing:** Simple card. "Club Access: $5/mo."
    
7.  **Footer:** SEO links, "Trusted Publisher" badge.
    

## 5.2 “Vault” Browsing Principles

-   **Grid:** Responsive. 2 columns on mobile (large enough to see details), 3-4 columns on desktop.
    
-   **Filters:** Horizontal scrollable chips on mobile (e.g., "Animals", "Space", "Fine Motor"). Sticky top bar.
    
-   **Empty States:** Playful. "No dinos here yet! Try 'Space'?" with a cute spot illustration.
    
-   **Tags:** Visible on cards but subtle (Small text, grey background).
    

## 5.3 Item Detail Page

-   **Layout:** Left: Large Preview Image (Watermarked). Right: Title, Description, "Download" Button.
    
-   **Watermark:** "HuePress Preview" tiled diagonally or a central logo overlay. Must obscure usage but allow evaluation.
    
-   **Trust Cues:** "Vector PDF," "A4 Size," "Bold Lines Guaranteed."
    
-   **Conversion:**
    
    -   _Free User:_ "Download" button -> Opens "Join the Club" Modal.
        
    -   _Subscriber:_ "Download" button -> Direct file download.
        

## 5.4 Accessibility & “Therapy-Grade” UX

-   **Contrast:** All text meets AA (4.5:1). Primary buttons are AAA against white.
    
-   **Touch Targets:** Minimum `48px` for all interactive elements (Mobile Mom friendly).
    
-   **Labels:** Descriptive. No "Click Here." Use "Download PDF."
    
-   **Focus States:** Clearly visible `3px` ring (Lilac or Blue) for keyboard navigation.
    

## 5.5 Trust Signals

-   **"Vector Quality" Badge:** Near every download button.
    
-   **"Ad-Free" Badge:** In the pricing section.
    
-   **"Curated by HuePress" Stamp:** In the footer.

# 7\. Brand Consistency Checklist

## 12 Do’s

1.  **Do** use Lora for headings to signal authority.
    
2.  **Do** use generous white space to let the art breathe.
    
3.  **Do** use thick (2px) borders on UI elements.
    
4.  **Do** focus on "Bold & Easy" as the core differentiator.
    
5.  **Do** use "Teal" for action and "Coral" for joy.
    
6.  **Do** write copy that sounds like a calm, helpful friend.
    
7.  **Do** optimize thumbnails for mobile scrolling.
    
8.  **Do** mention "Therapy-Grade" features (lines, motor skills).
    
9.  **Do** keep the path to download as short as possible.
    
10.  **Do** use real photos of printed pages for lifestyle shots.
     
11.  **Do** ensure all text has high contrast.
     
12.  **Do** make "Join the Club" the clear solution to friction.
     

## 12 Don’ts

1.  **Don't** use thin, delicate fonts (hard to read).
    
2.  **Don't** use primary red/blue/yellow (looks cheap/school).
    
3.  **Don't** crowd the interface with too many buttons.
    
4.  **Don't** use generic stock photos of smiling children.
    
5.  **Don't** use marketing hype ("Game-changer," "Synergy").
    
6.  **Don't** make the user click more than 3 times to print.
    
7.  **Don't** use gray backgrounds for the coloring pages.
    
8.  **Don't** clutter the view with ads or pop-ups.
    
9.  **Don't** use "Click Here" (be descriptive).
    
10.  **Don't** make the watermark obscure the entire image.
     
11.  **Don't** ignore the OT audience (keep it functional).
     
12.  **Don't** be screechy or alarmist in the copy.
